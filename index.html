1. Abstract - Research Overview

The Challenge: Diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs) affect millions globally, leading to 84% of diabetes-related amputations with an annual economic burden exceeding $9 billion.

Current Problem

Existing treatments are passive and fail to address the complex pathophysiology of chronic inflammation, impaired angiogenesis, and biofilm formation.

Our Innovation

A 4D-bioprinted, electrically-conductive scaffold that autonomously delivers engineered plant nanovesicles in response to wound conditions.

Goal

Create a "sense-and-respond" system that reprograms the diabetic wound environment through spatiotemporally-controlled therapy.

The Pathological Triad of Diabetic Wounds:

Chronic Inflammation

Persistent M1 macrophage polarization creates a hostile healing environment with excessive protease activity that destroys growth factors.

Impaired Angiogenesis

Reduced blood vessel formation leads to poor oxygen and nutrient delivery, starving the wound tissue.

Biofilm Formation

Protected bacterial communities resist antibiotics and perpetuate inflammation.

Current Limitations: Even advanced treatments like chitosan membranes or simple PDNV hydrogels use static delivery mechanisms - they can't adapt to the changing wound environment or coordinate the healing cascade.

4. Research Aims & Objectives

Overall Aim:

To fabricate and characterize a multi-functional, electroactive 4D-printed scaffold for triggered and sequential delivery of engineered PDNVs to accelerate diabetic wound healing.

Four Key Objectives:

Objective 1

Develop library of engineered PDNVs and stimuli-responsive nanoparticle carriers

Objective 2

Formulate, 3D/4D print, and characterize conductive hybrid hydrogel scaffold

Objective 3

Evaluate biological efficacy of scaffold in vitro

Objective 4

Evaluate therapeutic efficacy in diabetic mouse wound model
